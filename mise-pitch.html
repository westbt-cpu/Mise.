Why it exists

I built this because
I needed it.

Our pantry is split across three locations — kitchen shelves, a basement storage area, and the freezer. My spouse and I shop independently. We were constantly buying duplicates, running out of things we thought we had, and throwing away food that quietly expired.

Every app I tried was too complicated, required a subscription, or was designed for restaurants. mise. brings the chef's concept of mise en place — everything in its place, prepared before you need it — to the home kitchen.

It's a single webpage. Lives on your home screen. No download, no account, no subscription. Just open it and your kitchen is there.

Setup

Running in five minutes.

No app store. No sign-up. The fastest way in is a photo of your last grocery haul.

1
Open the link on your phone
Visit westbt-cpu.github.io/mise in Chrome. Tap Share → Add to Home Screen. mise. appears on your home screen instantly — nothing was installed.
2
Import your first haul in 90 seconds
Photograph your pantry or a recent grocery haul in Claude. Paste the list into mise. and it imports everything at once — no entering items one by one.
3
Organise by location
Assign items to where they actually live — Pantry, Fridge, Freezer, Basement. mise. shows you each location as a live shelf, not just a flat list.
4
Share the URL with your household
Send the link to anyone who shares your kitchen. They open it on their phone. No account required for either of you.
5
Tap +/− as you cook and shop
Update quantities as you use things. The restock list builds itself from everything below threshold. Bring it to the store.

📸 The fastest way to build your inventory

Most apps die at setup because entering 80 items manually takes an hour. mise. skips that entirely with haul import.

1
Photograph your pantry or grocery haul and send it to Claude: "List every item you can see, one per line: item name, quantity."
2
Copy Claude's response and paste it into the Import tab in mise.
3
Review each item, set location and category, hit apply. 30 items in under 90 seconds.
